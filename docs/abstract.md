## BodyPrompt: Prompting as Choreographic Search

BodyPrompt is a practice-based artistic research project that investigates
prompting as a form of choreographic search. Rather than treating prompts as
one-off instructions for generating movement, the project explores prompting as
an iterative dialogue in which human intention and generative AI progressively
search for a movement that resonates with a poetic theme.

The lecture performance demonstrates this search process live. Beginning with a
short poetic phrase, BodyPrompt generates multiple movement interpretations using
AI motion-generation models. These movements are visualised as animated stick
figures and movement notation rather than realistic human avatars, foregrounding
movement itself as the primary material of inquiry. As each generation suggests
new expressive possibilities, both the prompt and the generated movement are
continuously refined. The evolving sequence of prompts becomes a visible record
of the creative process, revealing not a linear workflow but an expanding
landscape of possibilities.

Rather than evaluating the correctness or faithfulness of AI-generated movement,
BodyPrompt embraces the variability of generative systems as a creative resource.
Each new generation becomes an opportunity to discover unexpected qualities of
movement and to refine the search towards an expression that better embodies the
intended poetic theme. Human intention and AI generation therefore co-evolve
through an open-ended process of exploration, where neither fully determines the
outcome.

BodyPrompt proposes prompting not as command or control, but as an embodied search
practice. Rather than presenting a new motion-generation system, the project
contributes a new way of thinking about how humans and generative AI can search
together for expressive movement. Through this live process of iterative prompting,
movement generation and reflection, the lecture performance reframes prompting
itself as a choreographic practice in which language, movement and computation
continuously shape one another.
